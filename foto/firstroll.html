Hello!

I'm Liam Brincat, a photographer born and raised on the island of Malta. I likely picked up my first ever camera before the age of 3; however, it took me 18 years to really take a photo. My first camera was a Halina Paulette trashcam; my first roll was Fomapan 100; my first adventure was in the city I then called home: Toulouse; my first shot was a brick wall. Click me if you'd like to see those photos and read more about my journey.

I've been hooked ever since that day. I document my life whenever I feel like, in the way I feel like; there isn't too much to it. Sometimes, I try to curate something more profound, reflecting my introspection outwards. Maybe you'll find them interesting.

My university classmate Shilong (@_davidlee805), a camera fanatic piques my interest with his craft. Months later, while rummaging through my grandma's stuff as she moves out, I find a beautiful Everest Mod.90 jet black maltese typewriter; a small chest of drawers for my desk; and a Halina Paulette. I was ecstatic, as to me, this find meant that I can shoot my first roll of film with Shilong. He bought me a roll of fomapan 100 until I got back to Toulouse. Once we decide on a day, we realise the came was broken. We open it up, tie a nice red string around the winding lever and get going. This first image is me after jerry rigging a fix and excited to get out.
